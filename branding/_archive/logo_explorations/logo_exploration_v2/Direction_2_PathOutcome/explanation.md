# Direction 2 — Path Outcome

**Phase:** Logo exploration v2 (concept)  
**Metaphor:** A path arrives at a resolved form.

---

## What you are looking at

A **horizontal stroke** (movement along a path) stops before a **solid rounded square** (outcome). The space between them is hesitation — not an arrow, not a connector line continuing through.

The curve was removed in favor of a straight path: fewer diagram cues, more **Vercel / Segment**-level reduction.

---

## Brand meaning alignment

| Stage | Expression in mark |
|-------|-------------------|
| Customer enters a path | Horizontal stroke |
| Customer hesitates | Stroke ends; does not touch the block |
| System decides / acts | Transition from open line to closed form |
| Outcome | Solid square — stable, finished |

Encodes **path → outcome** without flowchart boxes or API nodes.

---

## Design reference (not copy)

Studied **Vercel** and **Segment** for single-stroke simplicity. CartFlow adds a **deliberate stop** before resolution — the operational pause.

---

## Strengths

- Extremely **minimal** — one line, one shape  
- **Professional** and quiet on dashboards  
- Fast to reproduce in any medium  
- No literal commerce or messaging symbols  

---

## Weaknesses

- At small sizes, may resemble generic “dash + square” UI fragments  
- Less distinctive than Direction 1’s stroke/fill pairing  
- Story of “decision” is subtle — relies on copy context  
- Horizontal layout needs space in square app icons
