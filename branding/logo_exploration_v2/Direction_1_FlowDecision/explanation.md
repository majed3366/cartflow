# Direction 1 — Flow Decision

**Phase:** Logo exploration v2 (concept)  
**Metaphor:** Movement pauses; choice resolves into action.

---

## What you are looking at

Two forms separated by intentional space:

1. **Open pill (stroke)** — continuous flow, customer on a path  
2. **Solid pill (fill)** — resolved outcome after the system decides and acts  

The gap is not an arrow or connector. It is **hesitation** — the moment CartFlow exists to serve.

No carts, chat bubbles, nodes, or network lines.

---

## Brand meaning alignment

| Stage | Expression in mark |
|-------|-------------------|
| Customer enters a path | Open flowing form (left) |
| Customer hesitates | Deliberate gap between forms |
| System understands / decides | The break itself — choice before continuation |
| System acts / outcome | Solid resolved form (right) |

Maps to **Understand → Decide → Act → Recover** without illustrating internal architecture.

---

## Design reference (not copy)

Studied **Stripe** and **Linear** for geometric restraint: two shapes, one idea, no decoration. CartFlow’s version adds **pause as meaning** — the gap is the product.

---

## Strengths

- Reads as a **brand mark**, not a system diagram  
- Gap + solid pairing is **memorable** and ownable  
- Works at favicon scale (two simple rectangles)  
- No ecommerce or messaging association  
- Pairs cleanly with “CartFlow” wordmark  

---

## Weaknesses

- Gap may need tuning at very small sizes (16px)  
- Without wordmark, meaning is abstract (acceptable for SaaS)  
- Similar “two-block” language exists in other brands — differentiation relies on exact proportions and gap
